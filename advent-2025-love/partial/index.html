Advent Address

Love

1 John 4:7–10 (CSB)

7 Dear friends, let us love one another, because love is from God, and everyone who loves has been born of God and knows God. 8 The one who does not love does not know God, because God is love. 9 God’s love was revealed among us in this way: God sent his one and only Son into the world so that we might live through him. 10 Love consists in this: not that we loved God, but that he loved us and sent his Son to be the atoning sacrifice for our sins.

"not that we loved God, but that he loved us"

Can a newborn love? When a baby enters the world what are they able to do? Hardly anything! But when a baby is born to a loving mom and dad then what happens? The child is loved, and out of the love given to the child they develop an affection and love of their own toward Mom and Dad.

Do I want someone else's kids to love me in the way my kids do? No, that would be unnatural. I haven't bestowed love and grace on someone else's kid to the same degree that I have for my own. I certainly do not want any kids to go unloved, to be hungry or in pain, but also not all kids are mine and I can only keep my own house. In my house, one of my greatest desires is for my children to be so secure in my love for them that no matter what happens they will always find comfort in it.

So, to love God is only possible out of the love he shows you and me, just like a child will develop love for Mom and Dad as they are shown love. He shows grace to all, just like I will show grace to anyone else's kid - but God redeems his own just like at the end of the day I take my own kids home. Anyone who is not a child of God cannot love God - John says as much in what we just read together.

So, let's try to put on pause our concern for children that are not our own for a moment - and think of the love, concern, and grace we show our own kids. So it is with God and his love for us. If you do something grand, once, for someone do you expect unending love in return? Of course not... so it is with "common grace". God shows grace to all every day, but the ones who are his own children receive even more blessing. We receive the heart that can love God - the heart that is able to respond and develop that childlike affection for Mom or Dad.

Something that love brings, is freedom.

My kids don't get to do just whatever they want - but they're never more free than when they are enjoying life within the boundaries I have set for them, out of my deep love for them. My love for them enables them to be as free as reasonably possible... The epitome of freedom for the human is not "doing whatever I want", that is slavery to human flesh. No, true freedom is the gift of loving the Lord because loving him is the reason we were created - it's what we were made to do. His love for me frees me to want what he wants, love what he loves, hate what he hates, and ultimately to enjoy him forever.

I hope my kids love what I love, and hate what I hate because I hope that what I love and what I hate are in line with God's heart. And as God sent his own son into the world out of his love for you and me, may we go forward freed from the bondage of sin and death, into the glorious, true, and loving freedom that Jesus brings.
