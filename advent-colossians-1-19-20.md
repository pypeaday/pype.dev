content: "!!! note \"Colossians 1:19-20\"\n\n    19\_For God was pleased to have  all
  his fullness dwell in him,  20\_and through him to reconcile  everything to himself,
  \ whether things on earth or things in heaven,  by making peace  through his blood,
  shed on the cross.\n\nI feel like there's so much that could be unpacked here...\n\n-
  \"all his fullness dwell him in\" - so besides the entire Gospel of John how\n  can
  anyone deny that Jesus is God? I don't understand anyone who claims to take\n  the
  Bible as an authority and then deny Jesus' divinity.\n- \"through him to reconcile
  everything to himself\" - not all people are saved,\n  it's a clear reality. So
  what does it mean for **all things** to be\n  reconciled to God? Feels like that
  could go a lot of places.\n- \"making peace through his blood\" - before the cross
  there was an absence of\n  \"peace\", and through Jesus' blood peace has been made.
  Moreso than just that,\n  the \"make\" (eirenopoieo: \u03B5\u1F30\u03C1\u03B7\u03BD\u03BF\u03C0\u03BF\u03B9\u03AD\u03C9)
  is in the aorist which I understand to be\n  a tense that means a thing \"has happened\"
  and \"is done happening\". ie. it's a\n  completed action in the past.\n\n## KISS\n\nI'm
  going to keep-it-simple-silly here... I don't have the energy for arguments\nor
  unpacking some of these theological ideas that I genuinely do love. I found\nsome
  old notes on this exact passage in Logos about reconiliation of angels and\nsome
  grammar questions. However, for this Advent reflection I think there's\njust a simple
  truth to see, and it's one of the biggest points of the Story of\nthe Bible - Jesus
  makes peace, and he made peace through hard work, death.\n\nThe glory of the peace
  Jesus made is lost on anyone who doesn't recognize that\nthe world is broken. Jesus
  brings a new reality by unleashing Heaven to earth\nthrough his victory on the cross.
  We live in an interim \"already but not yet\"\nwhere the King Jesus is the rightful
  ruler over all creation, but he has yet to\ncome back and fully claim his own. The
  peace he made is a gift to all of\ncreation, for all of creation is his to reconcile.\n\nIf
  Jesus calls your name, what are you going to do? What can you do? He\ndefeated death
  and holds the universe together... you think you can say \"no\"?\nWhat about Jesus
  _not_ calling the name of someone in your life? Is peace not\nafforded to them?
  The Biblical truth is that it's fully and totally up to Yahweh\nto give his own
  to the Lord Jesus, and to keep each one without losing a hair\non their head. Those
  who are not of his kingdom will get what they naturally\nwant in the end. What that
  **actually is** I do not know, but it is not\neternity in the presence of a Good
  King because if God hasn't chosen you, you\ncan't love the Good King. If God hasn't
  chosen you - you can't **want** the\nGood King. In the end, we all get what we want
  - the benefit of\nthose chosen by Yahweh is that we are freed to want what is actually
  good for\nus. We are freed to love and live according to the purposes of our design.\n\nToday
  I pray Jesus impresses the reality of peace, and the freedom to want the\npeace
  he made, deeper into my heart, amen."

!!! note "Colossians 1:19-20"

    19 For God was pleased to have  all his fullness dwell in him,  20 and through him to reconcile  everything to himself,  whether things on earth or things in heaven,  by making peace  through his blood, shed on the cross.

I feel like there's so much that could be unpacked here...

- "all his fullness dwell him in" - so besides the entire Gospel of John how
  can anyone deny that Jesus is God? I don't understand anyone who claims to take
  the Bible as an authority and then deny Jesus' divinity.
- "through him to reconcile everything to himself" - not all people are saved,
  it's a clear reality. So what does it mean for **all things** to be
  reconciled to God? Feels like that could go a lot of places.
- "making peace through his blood" - before the cross there was an absence of
  "peace", and through Jesus' blood peace has been made. Moreso than just that,
  the "make" (eirenopoieo: εἰρηνοποιέω) is in the aorist which I understand to be
  a tense that means a thing "has happened" and "is done happening". ie. it's a
  completed action in the past.

## KISS

I'm going to keep-it-simple-silly here... I don't have the energy for arguments
or unpacking some of these theological ideas that I genuinely do love. I found
some old notes on this exact passage in Logos about reconiliation of angels and
some grammar questions. However, for this Advent reflection I think there's
just a simple truth to see, and it's one of the biggest points of the Story of
the Bible - Jesus makes peace, and he made peace through hard work, death.

The glory of the peace Jesus made is lost on anyone who doesn't recognize that
the world is broken. Jesus brings a new reality by unleashing Heaven to earth
through his victory on the cross. We live in an interim "already but not yet"
where the King Jesus is the rightful ruler over all creation, but he has yet to
come back and fully claim his own. The peace he made is a gift to all of
creation, for all of creation is his to reconcile.

If Jesus calls your name, what are you going to do? What can you do? He
defeated death and holds the universe together... you think you can say "no"?
What about Jesus _not_ calling the name of someone in your life? Is peace not
afforded to them? The Biblical truth is that it's fully and totally up to Yahweh
to give his own to the Lord Jesus, and to keep each one without losing a hair
on their head. Those who are not of his kingdom will get what they naturally
want in the end. What that **actually is** I do not know, but it is not
eternity in the presence of a Good King because if God hasn't chosen you, you
can't love the Good King. If God hasn't chosen you - you can't **want** the
Good King. In the end, we all get what we want - the benefit of
those chosen by Yahweh is that we are freed to want what is actually good for
us. We are freed to love and live according to the purposes of our design.

Today I pray Jesus impresses the reality of peace, and the freedom to want the
peace he made, deeper into my heart, amen.
