Questions for Dad about the Navy

September 01, 2025
#navy #note

Submarine Life

  1. What did a typical day look like when the submarine was underway?
  2. How did the 18-hour day with 6-hour shifts affect everyone's sense of time?
  3. How did you and the crew keep busy or entertained during long patrols?
  4. Can you describe the living quarters? What was the hardest part about the limited space?
  5. What meals do you remember best?

The Job

  1. What systems were you directly responsible for as a machinist?
  2. How did your team monitor and maintain the reactor? Any emergency drills that stick out?
  3. What's the most challenging mechanical problem you dealt with on the sub?
  4. What kind of training did you receive before serving underwater? How did reality differ?
  5. What did it take to "earn your dolphins"?

Technology

  1. What pieces of technology or engineering challenges stand out from working with the reactor?
  2. How were water, air, and electricity produced and maintained?
  3. Did you work with any systems that have since been declassified?
  4. What mechanical or safety upgrades have been made since your time that you wish you'd had?

The Experience

  1. What kind of camaraderie did you develop with the crew?
  2. Did your team face any emergencies or close calls with the reactor?
  3. How did you cope with being underwater for long periods?
  4. Were there any traditions or unwritten rules among the machinists?
  5. What was the relationship like between machinists, electricians, and other crew?
  6. If you could go back, what advice would you give to someone starting as a machinist on a nuclear sub today?
