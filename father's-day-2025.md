  becoming a dad and raising two beautiful kids has affected my view\non, or matured
  my view of, my relationship with God and how He sees me.\nBecause, biblically speaking,
  the father metaphor is very common and prominent\nfor Yahweh. He's many things to
  His chosen people. But the theology of adoption\nmakes the father model and metaphor
  a really good one. And being a dad,\nI feel like I've learned a lot.\n\n# The Childlike
  Position Before God\n\nThis is pretty related to the last post of mine [[a-new-perspective-on-being-god-s-child]]
  \nwhere the crux of that post is that, as smart as we might think we\nare as educated
  theologians, in the eyes of God we're still just children. And\nI mean that we should
  think that our understanding of our complex topics, is\nstill the level of a child
  in relation to God. And so in that\nsame vein I'm asking \n\n!!! warning \"\"\n
  \   \"In what other ways am I like a child to God?\" \n\nSo again today, I'm making
  sure to process some of these thoughts.\n\n## Babies and Who Am I?\n\nI remember
  both of my kids as babies would get overtired, and they'd be so\ntired that they
  couldn't fall asleep. And I would look at them and go - \"this\ndoesn't make sense.
  Your body knows you need sleep, you know you need sleep,\nbut what does it mean
  to be too tired? You can't fall asleep. And you just yell\nand scream at me.\" But
  that baby doesn't know that I'm their dad, they don't\nknow anything. They're just
  overtired, and they're yelling and screaming. It's\njust my job to protect them
  in that moment, comfort them, whatever. And I\nthought, \"man, this just has to
  be how God sees me sometimes.\" Even though I've\nbeen walking with the Lord for
  decades there's got to be\ntimes when I'm just throwing a tantrum. \n\n!!! info
  \"And that's how God sees me\"\n    as a creature he loves, who's just too tired
  to take a nap. And it's a\n    humbling thing to think that that's really sometimes
  how God sees me.\n\n# Unconditional Love & Disobedience\n\nAs my kids have gotten
  older, I think that I've picked up on other truths\nof the Lord - mainly how he
  can love me even when I sin... Even when my daughters\ndisobey, it doesn't change
  my love for them. I just had an experience today with\nmy daughter who climbed up
  too high on a swing set on a trapeze bar and\ncouldn't get herself down. And although
  it got tense and there was some shouting to handle, I eventually walked her through
  a few things:\n\n1. I told her not to get up there, and she disobeyed.\n2. By disobeying,
  she put herself into a dangerous and scary situation.\n3. I would help her in her
  scary situation, but my help might not look like how she wants it to look\n4. The
  help I can provide is to not remove her from the situation, i.e. to pick her up
  and just save her, but I could help her climb down and give her instruction.\n5.
  Finally, she needs to choose to trust me, even though she'll still be scared, and
  she needs to choose to trust me, even when I'm not standing where\nshe wants me
  to stand, or putting my hand where she wants me to put my hand, so\nthat she feels
  safe. \n\n!!! info \"Guidance Over Rescue & The Need for Trust\"\n    I need her
  to know that she is safe, and to trust that I tell her she is\n    safe, even when
  she doesn't see it or feel it. And that is what God requests\n    and demands of
  me, is to trust him more than I might be fearful of my future,\n    financially,
  or of relational security, or whatever.\n\n## Acting in Obedience Despite Fear\n\nThat
  trust doesn't necessarily take away the fear, but it does mean that you\nbehave
  obediently and act in accordance with the reality, not with the fear.\nThe reality
  is that Yahweh loves and protects me, just as I love and protect my\ndaughter, even
  when she was scared on the trapeze bar.\n\n# End\n\nSo Father's Day is, for me,
  a good time to just reflect on what that means to\nbe a dad. I'm not going to celebrate
  and do anything crazy. My family isn't\ngoing to, because I don't want us to. But
  this is a nice reminder, perhaps I\nshould do this more purposefully, more often,
  is reflect on how\nGod teaches me about his love for me through giving me a deep
  love for my\nchildren."

# Intro

I just wanted to capture some thoughts on Father's
Day and how becoming a dad and raising two beautiful kids has affected my view
on, or matured my view of, my relationship with God and how He sees me.
Because, biblically speaking, the father metaphor is very common and prominent
for Yahweh. He's many things to His chosen people. But the theology of adoption
makes the father model and metaphor a really good one. And being a dad,
I feel like I've learned a lot.

# The Childlike Position Before God

This is pretty related to the last post of mine [[a-new-perspective-on-being-god-s-child]] 
where the crux of that post is that, as smart as we might think we
are as educated theologians, in the eyes of God we're still just children. And
I mean that we should think that our understanding of our complex topics, is
still the level of a child in relation to God. And so in that
same vein I'm asking 

!!! warning ""
    "In what other ways am I like a child to God?" 

So again today, I'm making sure to process some of these thoughts.

## Babies and Who Am I?

I remember both of my kids as babies would get overtired, and they'd be so
tired that they couldn't fall asleep. And I would look at them and go - "this
doesn't make sense. Your body knows you need sleep, you know you need sleep,
but what does it mean to be too tired? You can't fall asleep. And you just yell
and scream at me." But that baby doesn't know that I'm their dad, they don't
know anything. They're just overtired, and they're yelling and screaming. It's
just my job to protect them in that moment, comfort them, whatever. And I
thought, "man, this just has to be how God sees me sometimes." Even though I've
been walking with the Lord for decades there's got to be
times when I'm just throwing a tantrum. 

!!! info "And that's how God sees me"
    as a creature he loves, who's just too tired to take a nap. And it's a
    humbling thing to think that that's really sometimes how God sees me.

# Unconditional Love & Disobedience

As my kids have gotten older, I think that I've picked up on other truths
of the Lord - mainly how he can love me even when I sin... Even when my daughters
disobey, it doesn't change my love for them. I just had an experience today with
my daughter who climbed up too high on a swing set on a trapeze bar and
couldn't get herself down. And although it got tense and there was some shouting to handle, I eventually walked her through a few things:

1. I told her not to get up there, and she disobeyed.
2. By disobeying, she put herself into a dangerous and scary situation.
3. I would help her in her scary situation, but my help might not look like how she wants it to look
4. The help I can provide is to not remove her from the situation, i.e. to pick her up and just save her, but I could help her climb down and give her instruction.
5. Finally, she needs to choose to trust me, even though she'll still be scared, and she needs to choose to trust me, even when I'm not standing where
she wants me to stand, or putting my hand where she wants me to put my hand, so
that she feels safe. 

!!! info "Guidance Over Rescue & The Need for Trust"
    I need her to know that she is safe, and to trust that I tell her she is
    safe, even when she doesn't see it or feel it. And that is what God requests
    and demands of me, is to trust him more than I might be fearful of my future,
    financially, or of relational security, or whatever.

## Acting in Obedience Despite Fear

That trust doesn't necessarily take away the fear, but it does mean that you
behave obediently and act in accordance with the reality, not with the fear.
The reality is that Yahweh loves and protects me, just as I love and protect my
daughter, even when she was scared on the trapeze bar.

# End

So Father's Day is, for me, a good time to just reflect on what that means to
be a dad. I'm not going to celebrate and do anything crazy. My family isn't
going to, because I don't want us to. But this is a nice reminder, perhaps I
should do this more purposefully, more often, is reflect on how
God teaches me about his love for me through giving me a deep love for my
children.
