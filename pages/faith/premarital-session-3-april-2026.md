# Expectations, Intimacy, and Shared Life

## Purpose

To surface unspoken expectations and ground shared life; emotionally, spiritually, and physically, in service to the Lord and each other.

1 Corinthians 7:1–5; Hebrews 13:4; Proverbs 24:3–4; Ecclesiastes 4:9–12

## Themes

Many marital wounds come from unspoken expectations.

- Not just unspoken, but a pattern of letting your expectations be unmet can lead to strife.
- It's up to both people in the marriage to be on the lookout: remember, together you face unspoken expectations, you do not face each other's expectations, be united

Intimacy is covenantal and requires safety, communication, and grace.

- No one is safer to talk to than your spouse (within reason, I think there's a place for men's/women's group kind of stuff, pastoral counseling, etc. but in general no one should be safer to share something with than your spouse)

Faithfulness is sustained through shared rhythms and priorities.

- Patterns are powerful
- In our lives, I for sure notice when we slip into patterns of lack-of-intimacy or communication, and the longer you "get by" the easier it is to live that way
- "Missing something once is a mistake, missing something twice is the start of a new habit." The point being that it takes work to communicate, and you should plan to communicate often. Missing a date, or an agreed-upon-time to talk about something once is just a mistake, but don't let yourselves push off hard conversations (either together or individually)

## Discussion Prompts

### Expectations

What are you most looking forward to in daily married life?

What concerns you about sharing life so closely?

### Intimacy

How do you communicate needs, boundaries, and fears?

- with great trust in your spouse
- openly, hopefully.
- do not assume the first thing said is what is meant, maybe you need to work through it together to figure out what one of you needs

How will you protect intimacy after marriage?

### Shared Life

How do you approach money and financial decisions?

- Our approach: God gives us everything and we recognize that any ordered system requires a hierarchy of decision makers. I "make the final decision" which is often to trust what Kassia wants to do with some money. Kassia asks me about financial things, not for permission, but for information. Then from the information, we decide together and if we disagree it's up to me to put my foot down on it or defer to her in our disagreement (both circumstances happen)

What rhythms help you remain spiritually grounded?

## Mentor Contribution

Share principles, not details, regarding intimacy and finances.

Emphasize patience, mutual service, and long-term faithfulness.
