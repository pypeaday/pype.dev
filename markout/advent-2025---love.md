  \   7\_Dear friends, let us love one another, because love is from God, and everyone

  but that he loved us\"\n\nCan a newborn love? When a baby enters the world they
  are at the mercy of the,\nhopefully, loving mom and dad God gave to them. But, whatever
  circumstances\nsurround them - they didn't choose any of it. As the child is loved
  then\nout of that love they develop an affection and love of their own toward Mom
  and\nDad.\n\nSo, to love God is only possible out of the love he shows you and me,
  just like\na child will develop love for Mom and Dad as they are shown love.\n\nNot
  all people love God, but He does show grace to all.\n\n!!! note \"Palm 145:9\"\n\n
  \   Yahweh is good to all,  and his mercies are over all his works.\n\nAnd just
  like Yahweh is good to all, I will show grace to anyone else's kid -\nbut God redeems
  his own just like at the end of the day I take my own kids\nhome: my kids, who I
  loved before they could love me. Only my children can love\nme as their Dad, and
  anyone who is not a child of God cannot love God - John\nsays as much in what we
  just read together.\n\nBut love is more than affection, and even security. It is
  those things of\ncourse, but it's also more. Something else that love brings to
  the human life,\nis freedom.\n\nMy kids don't get to do just whatever they want
  - but they're never more free\nthan when they are enjoying life within the boundaries
  I have set for them. My\nlove for my kids enables them to be as free as reasonably
  possible... The\nepitome of freedom for the human is not \"doing whatever I want\",
  that is\nslavery to myself. If I treat myself as my own\nMaster, then I will be
  my own slave.\n\nTrue freedom is the gift of loving the Lord because loving him
  is the reason we\nwere created - it's what we were made to do. His love for me frees
  me to want\nwhat he wants, love what he loves, hate what he hates, and ultimately
  to enjoy\nhim forever.\n\nI hope my kids love what I love, and hate what I hate
  because I hope that what\nI love and what I hate are in line with God's heart. And
  as God sent his own\nson into the world out of his love for you and me, may we go
  forward freed from\nthe bondage of sin and death, into the glorious, true, and loving
  freedom that\nJesus brings.\n\n## Prayer\n\nGood morning, Father. Thank you for
  Love, thank you for Christmas. For those\nhere who have been freed to love you I
  pray this week and through Christmas we\nare drawn to consider the freedom we have
  in your love, the freedom to love\nyou, the freedom to choose what is pleasing to
  you and not what our\nsinful-selves demand. For those here still dead in their sins,
  unable to love\nyou, I pray you speak their name and call them to life and to freedom.
  We long\nfor your return as we celebrate your incarnation. Thank you for loving
  us\nbefore we could love you, now may we love you and others this week, in Jesus'\nname.
  Amen."

# Advent Address

## Love

!!! note "1 John 4:710 (CSB)"

    7Dear friends, let us love one another, because love is from God, and everyone who loves has been born of God and knows God. 8The one who does not love does not know God, because God is love. 9Gods love was revealed among us in this way: God sent his one and only Son into the world so that we might live through him. 10Love consists in this: not that we loved God, but that he loved us and sent his Son to be the atoning sacrifice for our sins.

"not that we loved God, but that he loved us"

Can a newborn love? When a baby enters the world they are at the mercy of the,
hopefully, loving mom and dad God gave to them. But, whatever circumstances
surround them - they didn't choose any of it. As the child is loved then
out of that love they develop an affection and love of their own toward Mom and
Dad.

So, to love God is only possible out of the love he shows you and me, just like
a child will develop love for Mom and Dad as they are shown love.

Not all people love God, but He does show grace to all.

!!! note "Palm 145:9"

    Yahweh is good to all,  and his mercies are over all his works.

And just like Yahweh is good to all, I will show grace to anyone else's kid -
but God redeems his own just like at the end of the day I take my own kids
home: my kids, who I loved before they could love me. Only my children can love
me as their Dad, and anyone who is not a child of God cannot love God - John
says as much in what we just read together.

But love is more than affection, and even security. It is those things of
course, but it's also more. Something else that love brings to the human life,
is freedom.

My kids don't get to do just whatever they want - but they're never more free
than when they are enjoying life within the boundaries I have set for them. My
love for my kids enables them to be as free as reasonably possible... The
epitome of freedom for the human is not "doing whatever I want", that is
slavery to myself. If I treat myself as my own
Master, then I will be my own slave.

True freedom is the gift of loving the Lord because loving him is the reason we
were created - it's what we were made to do. His love for me frees me to want
what he wants, love what he loves, hate what he hates, and ultimately to enjoy
him forever.

I hope my kids love what I love, and hate what I hate because I hope that what
I love and what I hate are in line with God's heart. And as God sent his own
son into the world out of his love for you and me, may we go forward freed from
the bondage of sin and death, into the glorious, true, and loving freedom that
Jesus brings.

## Prayer

Good morning, Father. Thank you for Love, thank you for Christmas. For those
here who have been freed to love you I pray this week and through Christmas we
are drawn to consider the freedom we have in your love, the freedom to love
you, the freedom to choose what is pleasing to you and not what our
sinful-selves demand. For those here still dead in their sins, unable to love
you, I pray you speak their name and call them to life and to freedom. We long
for your return as we celebrate your incarnation. Thank you for loving us
before we could love you, now may we love you and others this week, in Jesus'
name. Amen.
