  \   7\_Dear friends, let us love one another, because love is from God, and everyone
  who loves has been born of God and knows God. 8\_The one who does not love does
  not know God, because God is love. 9\_God\u2019s love was revealed among us in this
  way: God sent his one and only Son into the world so that we might live through
  him. 10\_Love consists in this: not that we loved God, but that he loved us and
  sent his Son to be the atoning sacrifice for our sins.\n\n\"not that we loved God,
  but that he loved us\"\n\nCan a newborn love? When a baby enters the world what
  are they able to do?\nHardly anything! But when a baby is born to a loving mom and
  dad then what\nhappens? The child is loved, and out of the love given to the child
  they\ndevelop an affection and love of their own toward Mom and Dad.\n\nDo I want
  someone else's kids to love me in the way my kids do? No, that would\nbe unnatural.
  I haven't bestowed love and grace on someone else's kid\nto the same degree that
  I have for my own. I certainly do not want any kids to\ngo unloved, to be hungry
  or in pain, but also not all kids are mine and I can\nonly keep my own house. In
  my house, one of my greatest desires is for my\nchildren to be so secure in my love
  for them that no matter what happens they\nwill always find comfort in it.\n\nSo,
  to love God is only possible out of the love he shows you and me, just like\na child
  will develop love for Mom and Dad as they are shown love. He shows\ngrace to all,
  just like I will show grace to anyone else's kid - but God\nredeems his own just
  like at the end of the day I take my own kids home. Anyone\nwho is not a child of
  God cannot love God - John says as much in what we just\nread together.\n\nSo, let's
  try to put on pause our concern for children that are not our own for\na moment
  - and think of the love, concern, and grace we show our own kids. So\nit is with
  God and his love for us. If you do something grand, once, for someone\ndo you expect
  unending love in return? Of course not... so it is with \"common\ngrace\". God shows
  grace to all every day, but the ones who are his own children\nreceive even more
  blessing. We receive the heart that can love God - the heart\nthat is able to respond
  and develop that childlike affection for Mom or Dad.\n\nSomething that love brings,
  is freedom.\n\nMy kids don't get to do just whatever they want - but they're never
  more free\nthan when they are enjoying life within the boundaries I have set for
  them, out\nof my deep love for them. My love for them enables them to be as free
  as\nreasonably possible... The epitome of freedom for the human is not \"doing\nwhatever
  I want\", that is slavery to human flesh. No, true freedom is the gift\nof loving
  the Lord because loving him is the reason we were created - it's what\nwe were made
  to do. His love for me frees me to want what he wants, love\nwhat he loves, hate
  what he hates, and ultimately to enjoy him forever.\n\nI hope my kids love what
  I love, and hate what I hate because I hope that what\nI love and what I hate are
  in line with God's heart. And as God sent his own\nson into the world out of his
  love for you and me, may we go forward freed from\nthe bondage of sin and death,
  into the glorious, true, and loving freedom that\nJesus brings."

# Advent Address

## Love

!!! note "1 John 4:7–10 (CSB)"

    7 Dear friends, let us love one another, because love is from God, and everyone who loves has been born of God and knows God. 8 The one who does not love does not know God, because God is love. 9 God’s love was revealed among us in this way: God sent his one and only Son into the world so that we might live through him. 10 Love consists in this: not that we loved God, but that he loved us and sent his Son to be the atoning sacrifice for our sins.

"not that we loved God, but that he loved us"

Can a newborn love? When a baby enters the world what are they able to do?
Hardly anything! But when a baby is born to a loving mom and dad then what
happens? The child is loved, and out of the love given to the child they
develop an affection and love of their own toward Mom and Dad.

Do I want someone else's kids to love me in the way my kids do? No, that would
be unnatural. I haven't bestowed love and grace on someone else's kid
to the same degree that I have for my own. I certainly do not want any kids to
go unloved, to be hungry or in pain, but also not all kids are mine and I can
only keep my own house. In my house, one of my greatest desires is for my
children to be so secure in my love for them that no matter what happens they
will always find comfort in it.

So, to love God is only possible out of the love he shows you and me, just like
a child will develop love for Mom and Dad as they are shown love. He shows
grace to all, just like I will show grace to anyone else's kid - but God
redeems his own just like at the end of the day I take my own kids home. Anyone
who is not a child of God cannot love God - John says as much in what we just
read together.

So, let's try to put on pause our concern for children that are not our own for
a moment - and think of the love, concern, and grace we show our own kids. So
it is with God and his love for us. If you do something grand, once, for someone
do you expect unending love in return? Of course not... so it is with "common
grace". God shows grace to all every day, but the ones who are his own children
receive even more blessing. We receive the heart that can love God - the heart
that is able to respond and develop that childlike affection for Mom or Dad.

Something that love brings, is freedom.

My kids don't get to do just whatever they want - but they're never more free
than when they are enjoying life within the boundaries I have set for them, out
of my deep love for them. My love for them enables them to be as free as
reasonably possible... The epitome of freedom for the human is not "doing
whatever I want", that is slavery to human flesh. No, true freedom is the gift
of loving the Lord because loving him is the reason we were created - it's what
we were made to do. His love for me frees me to want what he wants, love
what he loves, hate what he hates, and ultimately to enjoy him forever.

I hope my kids love what I love, and hate what I hate because I hope that what
I love and what I hate are in line with God's heart. And as God sent his own
son into the world out of his love for you and me, may we go forward freed from
the bondage of sin and death, into the glorious, true, and loving freedom that
Jesus brings.
