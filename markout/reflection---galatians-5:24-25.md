  Christ\u230B\u2009 have crucified the flesh together with its feelings and its desires.\n
  \   25\_If we live by the Spirit, we must also follow the Spirit. 26\_We must not
  become conceited, provoking one another, envying one another.\n\nSpurgeon's \"Morning
  and Evening\" is a mediation on living in and following the Spirit. He says this:\n\n>
  \ You will never find true faith unattended by true godliness; on the other\n>  hand,
  you will never discover a truly holy life which has not for its root a\n>  living
  faith upon the righteousness of Christ\n\n`godliness` is the \"following the Spirit\"
  in our daily lives - it is living\n\"like god\". What then is \"true faith\"? I
  think I can take Spurgeon to be saying\nthat upon internal meditation do I find
  my faith to be secure and genuine.\n\"true faith\" is between me and the Lord.\n\nThe
  meditation this morning though... \"If I live by the Spirit, I must also\nfollow
  the Spirit\" is once again heavy on my heart as I wrestled through\n[[reflecting-on-romans-7-7-13]]
  a few weeks ago - the reality that I daily\nchoose things contrary to my faith.
  \n\nI think Paul is saying \"because you have crucified the flesh\" as the parallel\npoint
  to \"we must also follow the Spirit\". He's saying \"if you say you've\ncrucified
  the flesh, show your faith by following as well\" - it's James' \"show\nme your
  faith by what you do\"\n\nSo who am I today? Whom will I serve?\n\nThese morning
  meditations are like my 100x push-ups a day... the days I do\nthem just seem marginally
  better, and in a season of consistency the value\nseems to compound... I thank Jesus
  for the conviction in the first place to\nstrive for holiness, and for the ~~strength~~
  faith to say no to the flesh, but\nyes to the Spirit."

!!! note "Galatians 5:24-25"

    24 Now those ⌊who belong to Christ⌋  have crucified the flesh together with its feelings and its desires.
    25 If we live by the Spirit, we must also follow the Spirit. 26 We must not become conceited, provoking one another, envying one another.

Spurgeon's "Morning and Evening" is a mediation on living in and following the Spirit. He says this:

>  You will never find true faith unattended by true godliness; on the other
>  hand, you will never discover a truly holy life which has not for its root a
>  living faith upon the righteousness of Christ

`godliness` is the "following the Spirit" in our daily lives - it is living
"like god". What then is "true faith"? I think I can take Spurgeon to be saying
that upon internal meditation do I find my faith to be secure and genuine.
"true faith" is between me and the Lord.

The meditation this morning though... "If I live by the Spirit, I must also
follow the Spirit" is once again heavy on my heart as I wrestled through
[[reflecting-on-romans-7-7-13]] a few weeks ago - the reality that I daily
choose things contrary to my faith. 

I think Paul is saying "because you have crucified the flesh" as the parallel
point to "we must also follow the Spirit". He's saying "if you say you've
crucified the flesh, show your faith by following as well" - it's James' "show
me your faith by what you do"

So who am I today? Whom will I serve?

These morning meditations are like my 100x push-ups a day... the days I do
them just seem marginally better, and in a season of consistency the value
seems to compound... I thank Jesus for the conviction in the first place to
strive for holiness, and for the ~~strength~~ faith to say no to the flesh, but
yes to the Spirit.
