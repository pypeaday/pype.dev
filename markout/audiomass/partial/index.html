#sermons #tech

I edit our church's recorded sermons every week and upload them to our website. It's very simple, and the editing is just cutting the audio file down on the front and back sides of extra stuff picked up while the mic was on. I've been doing this for a while using Davinci Resolve which is WAY too heavy for trimming audio files but I'm loosely familiar with it and so I just have been dealing with the pain of long start up, overly complicated organization, etc. Well it's been buggy on Aurora for me and a few weeks ago I got sick of it finally and tried KdenLive.... Now this is a great tool, but again I'm just cutting up some audio files, why is this hard?

Double finally I just did some research and after a few minutes found audiomass which I immediately forked and dockerized cause it's so simple and beautiful!

Now I have a little webapp to trim these files from - almost such that the tech lead on Sunday could maybe do this instead of me!

You can check it out on my github

  • clone
  • docker compose up -d
  • http://localhost:5056 to begin trimming audio locally!
